# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Provides status updates, escalates blockers, collaborates on planning and estimates
- **Product Managers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria, provides technical feedback on feasibility
- **Solution Architect**: Consults on design decisions, follows architectural standards and patterns
- **Business Analyst**: Works closely to understand detailed requirements and validate implementations
- **Quality Advocate**: Collaborates on test strategy, reviews test coverage, pairs on test automation
- **UX Designer**: Partners on UI implementation, provides feedback on design feasibility
- **Scrum Master**: Raises blockers and impediments, participates in agile ceremonies

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on requirements clarification, validates technical solutions, reviews completed features
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on roadmap priorities, release schedules, and stakeholder communication
- **Business Analyst**: Partners on detailed requirements definition, user story refinement, and acceptance criteria
- **Solution Architect**: Discusses technical feasibility and trade-offs for roadmap items
- **UX Designer**: Collaborates on feature requirements, validates user needs and design solutions
- **Quality Advocate**: Ensures quality requirements are prioritized in the backlog
- **Scrum Master**: Works together to refine product backlog and ensure sprint readiness
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates on release content, timing, and stakeholder communication

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Tracks progress, removes organizational blockers, coordinates resource allocation
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on priorities, manages scope and timeline trade-offs, coordinates stakeholder communication
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on delivery schedules, risk management, and process improvements
- **Solution Architect**: Works together on scoping, effort estimation, and technical risk management
- **Business Analyst**: Ensures requirements are clear and complete before planning
- **Quality Advocate**: Aligns on quality goals, acceptance criteria, and release readiness
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates release schedules, communication plans, and stakeholder updates

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and coach the team on process improvements. They ensure the team follows agile principles and continuously evolves their practices for better delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments and blockers that slow down the team
- Coach the team on agile practices and self-organization
- Shield the team from external distractions and interruptions
- Track and visualize team velocity and workflow metrics
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Reduce cycle time and eliminate waste
- Build a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Ensure transparent communication and collaboration

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to identify and address blockers
- Weekly syncs with Project Managers to resolve delivery issues
- Sprint retrospectives to drive process improvements
- Regular coaching sessions with team members

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on delivery schedules, risk management, and status reporting; escalates organizational blockers
- **Product Managers**: Ensures product backlog is refined and ready for sprint planning; facilitates clarification of acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes technical and process blockers, facilitates collaboration
- **Quality Advocate**: Partners on improving testing practices and quality metrics within sprints

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and refine requirements, mapping business needs to product features. They bridge the gap between stakeholders and the development team by ensuring requirements are clear, complete, and actionable.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Create detailed user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Validate that delivered features meet business needs
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation
- Facilitate workshops and requirement gathering sessions

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Minimize rework due to unclear or changing requirements
- Maximize alignment between business needs and technical solutions
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction through effective communication

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story refinement sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Acceptance criteria reviews and clarifications
- Business process documentation and workflow diagrams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on roadmap priorities, translates vision into detailed requirements, validates feature designs
- **Developers**: Works closely to clarify acceptance criteria, answer questions during implementation, validate technical solutions
- **Stakeholders**: Gathers requirements, validates assumptions, and ensures business needs are met
- **Quality Advocate**: Defines testable acceptance criteria and assists with test scenario development

---

## Solution Architect

### Role Summary
Solution Architects design technical solutions and ensure scalable, maintainable architectures. They provide technical leadership and ensure that implementation decisions align with long-term architectural goals.

### Responsibilities
- Design technical architecture and solution patterns
- Evaluate technology choices and make recommendations
- Define technical standards and best practices
- Review and approve architectural decisions
- Identify technical risks and dependencies
- Ensure solutions are scalable, secure, and maintainable
- Create architectural documentation and diagrams

### Goals
- Deliver robust, scalable technical solutions
- Minimize technical debt and architectural complexity
- Ensure consistency across technical implementations
- Balance short-term delivery needs with long-term architecture goals

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review sessions and design discussions
- Technical design documents and architecture diagrams
- Weekly syncs with Project Managers on technical risks
- Code reviews focused on architectural alignment

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides technical guidance, reviews design proposals, mentors on architectural patterns
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on scoping, effort estimation, and technical risk management
- **Product Managers**: Advises on technical feasibility and trade-offs for roadmap items
- **Quality Advocate**: Ensures architecture supports testability and quality objectives

---

## User Experience (UX) Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers champion usability, accessibility, and user research. They translate user needs and product requirements into intuitive, accessible designs that delight users and meet business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Ensure designs meet accessibility standards (WCAG)
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Validate designs with users and stakeholders
- Advocate for user needs in product discussions
- Collaborate on design reviews and iterations

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate designs through user research and testing
- Ensure consistency across product experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and insights presentations
- Weekly syncs with Product Managers on feature designs
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature requirements, translates vision into design prototypes, validates user needs
- **Developers**: Works together on implementation feasibility, provides design specifications and assets, reviews implemented UI
- **Business Analysts**: Incorporates business requirements into design solutions, validates user workflows
- **Quality Advocate**: Partners on usability testing and accessibility validation

## Quality Advocate

### Role Summary
Quality Advocates focus on ongoing test strategy, automation, and QA practices beyond feature-level testing. They champion quality throughout the development lifecycle and help teams build quality into their processes.

### Responsibilities
- Define and evolve test strategy and quality standards
- Build and maintain test automation frameworks
- Identify quality risks and gaps in test coverage
- Coach team members on testing best practices
- Monitor quality metrics and trends
- Conduct exploratory testing and risk-based testing
- Ensure non-functional requirements are tested (performance, security, accessibility)

### Goals
- Maximize test coverage and effectiveness
- Shift quality left by catching issues early
- Build a culture of quality ownership across the team
- Reduce production defects and improve reliability

### Typical Communication
- Quality reviews during sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Test reports and coverage metrics
- Risk assessments for upcoming releases
- Pairing sessions with Developers on test automation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Partners on test strategy, reviews test coverage, coaches on testing practices and automation
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on quality goals, acceptance criteria, and release readiness across sprints
- **Product Managers**: Ensures quality requirements are understood and prioritized in the backlog
- **Release Manager**: Collaborates on release testing, smoke tests, and go/no-go decisions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployments, release notes, and communication with stakeholders. They ensure releases are well-planned, properly communicated, and can be safely rolled back if needed.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases across environments
- Coordinate release activities with cross-functional teams
- Prepare and publish release notes and documentation
- Conduct pre-release readiness reviews
- Execute deployment procedures and runbooks
- Monitor post-deployment health and metrics
- Manage rollback procedures when needed
- Maintain release calendars and communication plans

### Goals
- Execute safe, predictable releases with minimal disruption
- Ensure stakeholders are informed about release contents and timing
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Maintain comprehensive release documentation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM and engineering teams
- Pre-release go/no-go reviews with stakeholders
- Deployment status updates during release windows
- Post-release reports and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on release schedules, communicates risks, ensures project deliverables are release-ready
- **Quality Advocate**: Partners on release testing strategy, smoke tests, and validation of release criteria
- **Developers**: Collaborates on deployment procedures, troubleshooting, and rollback plans
- **Stakeholders**: Provides release notes, deployment schedules, and impact communications

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Cross-functional collaboration is key: most work requires multiple personas working together with clear hand-offs and communication.
